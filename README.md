# AI Diagnostic System Implementation
---

##  Project Overview

This project focuses on the **implementation of an AI-powered diagnostic support system** in a healthcare laboratory setting. The objective was to enhance diagnostic efficiency, reduce manual workload, and improve turnaround time through structured project execution and stakeholder alignment.

---

##  Business Problem

Diagnostic laboratories often face:

* Delays in report turnaround time (TAT)
* High dependency on manual processes
* Increased risk of diagnostic errors under workload pressure
* Scalability challenges with growing patient volume

These inefficiencies impact both **patient satisfaction** and **operational performance**.

---

##  Project Management Approach

A **Hybrid (Agile + Waterfall)** methodology was used:

* **Waterfall** → For planning, approvals, and compliance-driven processes
* **Agile** → For iterative development, testing, and feedback cycles

This ensured a balance between **structured execution** and **flexibility for improvement**.

##  Key Challenges & Solutions

### 1. Stakeholder Resistance

**Challenge:** Technicians were hesitant due to fear of AI replacing their roles
**Solution:**

* Involved them early in pilot testing
* Positioned AI as a support tool
* Incorporated their feedback

---

### 2. Integration Delays

**Challenge:** API compatibility issues slowed progress
**Solution:**

* Broke tasks into smaller milestones
* Increased coordination with IT and vendor

---

### 3. AI Accuracy Concerns

**Challenge:** Initial inconsistencies in AI outputs
**Solution:**

* Introduced validation layer with pathologists
* Conducted iterative testing and refinement

---

##  Key Outcomes

*  **25% reduction in turnaround time**
* **Improved diagnostic accuracy (up to 93%)**
* **80%+ user adoption rate**
*  **Enhanced workflow efficiency and reduced manual effort**
